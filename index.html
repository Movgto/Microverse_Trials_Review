Metroid Dread

Metroid Dread had become a near-mythic game, a fabled DS entry that never saw the light of day by the time it was announced at E3 2021 as a Switch game.

With nearly 20 years having passed since the last new 2D Metroid, expectations were sky high. Fortunately for fans, Dread met (and in many cases surpassed) expectations for what a modern 2D Metroid game could accomplish.

Notable features
  • It’s easily the smoothest game in the series, running at a silky 60fps
  • Incorporates several new weapons and abilities the series now can’t live without
  • high-production values, incredible game design that gently guides the player through ZDR’s labyrinthine corridors, and the most unflinching version of Samus we’ve seen yet
Animal Crossing

Released on the doorstep of a global pandemic, Animal Crossing: New Horizons provided a much need escape to many, selling nearly 34 million copies to date.

Routine and discovery play equally important roles as you plan the perfect layout for your island, make friends (or enemies) with all your villagers, and invite your friends to your own little utopia to trade items and swap secrets.

Notable features
  • It’s brilliant in its simplicity and masterful in the way it encourages players to keep up with chores.
  • The writing is supremely funny and that, hundreds of hours in, you’re still able to chuckle at a random comment or find genuine inspiration in the places you’d least expect.
Super Mario Odissey

A masterclass in 3D platforming, Super Mario Odyssey seamlessly blends the best elements from nearly every Mario game with an entire portfolio of new gameplay mechanics to create something both nostalgic and courageous.

Notable features

  • New players will adore stomping through the vivid and vast new worlds, while seasoned veterans will stick around after the credits to unlock the hundreds of challenges that await their skill and dexterity.
  • Super Mario Odyssey is pure, sublime joy and one of the best Super Mario games ever made
Super Smash Bros

Super Smash Bros. Ultimate is exactly what its name implies: it’s the ultimate incarnation of Nintendo’s now 20-year-old brawler series. It’s a celebration of Smash Bros. as a whole, filled with more fighters and levels than ever before, and packed to the gills with over 1000 more characters from all across gaming.

Notable features

  • 20+ hour single-player mode with full-on boss fights and huge world maps.
  • “Everyone is here!” may have started out as just another tagline, but it’s one that Nintendo has impressively backed up
The Legend Of Zelda

The ultimate Best Nintendo Switch game is The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild. Let’s face it, the Zelda series was long overdue for a major change, and The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild offers an unparalleled sense of freedom and scale in the palm of your hand.

Notable features

  • Tells an epic story, as you glide, cook, and battle your way across a beautifully ruined version of Hyrule.
